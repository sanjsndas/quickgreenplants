🍪 Cookies & Green Data Practices

We use cookies to cultivate a better browsing experience — just like compost enriches soil. Learn how we keep your data fresh and transparent.

🌱 What Are Cookies?

Cookies are small text files placed on your device when you visit Quick Green Plants. They help us remember your preferences, understand how you interact with our organic gardening content, and improve our 78,000+ word library experience. Think of them as little seed packets that help our site grow smarter for you.

We respect your privacy and believe in full transparency. This policy explains which cookies we use, why we use them, and how you can manage your choices.

📋 Types of Cookies We Use

Below you'll find the specific cookies active on Quick Green Plants. Each serves a distinct purpose, from essential site function to enhancing your gardening journey.

🌿 Your Green Choice: We never use invasive third-party tracking. Any analytics data is aggregated and anonymized to protect your identity, just like companion planting protects your veggies.

⚖️ Your Cookie Choices & Consent

When you first visit Quick Green Plants, you'll see a simple cookie banner. You can accept all essential + optional cookies, or customize your preferences. You can update or withdraw consent anytime by clicking the "Cookie Preferences" button below or via our floating leaf icon (visible at bottom-right on all pages).

🍪 Manage Cookie Settings

Select which cookie categories you're comfortable with. Essential cookies are always active to ensure our contact forms, login-free experience, and security work properly.

Any changes will apply immediately to your current session and future visits. Your preferences are stored in a "consent_cookie" for 12 months.

🛠️ How to Disable Cookies via Browser

Most modern browsers allow you to block or delete cookies entirely. However, doing so may affect the functionality of our gardening tools, like saving your plant hardiness zone. You can find instructions in your browser’s help section (Chrome, Firefox, Safari, Edge). Alternatively, use the “Do Not Track” setting — we honor DNT signals as a sign of respect for your privacy.

🌐 Third-Party Services & Embedded Content

Our website may embed videos from YouTube (for gardening tutorials), Instagram posts (community highlights), and provide links to ethical seed libraries. These services may set their own cookies when you interact with them. We do not control those third-party cookies, but we recommend reviewing their policies. Examples:

  • 🍅 YouTube – for workshop replays and "how-to" compost videos (may set cookies for playback preferences).
  • 📌 Pinterest & Instagram – for plant inspiration galleries (cookies for social sharing).
  • 📧 Email service (Mailchimp) – if you subscribe to our Green Thumb Newsletter, tracking pixels help us understand engagement.

We only use trusted partners who comply with GDPR, CCPA, and other privacy frameworks. You can always block third-party cookies through your browser.
